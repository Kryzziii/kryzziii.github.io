Abstract

The growing popularity of online games increases the demand for reliable, low-latency game servers. At Nitrado, monitoring is still mainly passive and pull-based, which can miss short load spikes and delays automated countermeasures. This thesis therefore designs and prototypes a proactive push-based monitoring architecture. On the host side, osquery performs local analysis with scheduled SQL queries, while critical states are sent asynchronously to Apache Kafka. The Go-based consumer processes these events and triggers responses through the company's proprietary API.

Result & conclusion

The local proof-of-concept confirms that the functional requirements were met and that the core components remain highly resource-efficient. Overall, the work shows that proactive monitoring can reduce response times and form a solid basis for automated mitigation in scalable server environments.

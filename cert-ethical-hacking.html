Training Curriculum

Successfully completed the Ethical Hacking university module (4 ECTS, corresponding to a 120-hour workload), conducted in Summer 2025 by Florian Dalwigk.

The final examination rigorously tested these skills through a practical hacking challenge, the preparation of a professional penetration testing report, and a written exam (Final Grade: 1.0).

Key areas of expertise include:

  • Frameworks & Methodology: Cyber Kill Chain, MITRE ATT&CK, CVE/CVSS, Operational Security, and Legal Foundations.
  • Reconnaissance & Exploitation: Scanning, Enumeration, Privilege Escalation, and utilizing tools like Nmap, Shodan, Metasploit, BurpSuite, and sqlmap.
  • Web & System Attacks: OWASP Top 10, SQL Injection, XSS, Buffer Overflows, Malware, and Password Hacking (Hashcat, Hydra).
  • AI in Ethical Hacking: Exploring LLM vulnerabilities (OWASP Top 10 LLM) and utilizing AI-driven tools like ShellGPT and Ollama.
  • Professional Execution: Understanding Social Engineering tactics and creating professional Pentest Reports.
